# How to Fire an Underperforming Offshore Software Agency

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** firing offshore developers, changing IT vendors, software vendor transition

It is the nightmare scenario for any CTO or founder. 

You hired an offshore software development agency six months ago to build your core enterprise platform. At first, everything was great. But now, the code is littered with bugs. The developers are missing deadlines. The Tech Lead has stopped replying to your emails promptly. 

You realize you have to fire the agency. 

But you cannot just send an angry email and cut off their access. If you do that abruptly, the agency might retaliate. They might withhold critical passwords, refuse to transfer the domain names, or leave you with a completely undocumented codebase that your next developers cannot read. 

Firing a software vendor is a delicate, strategic operation. Here is how to execute a safe "Vendor Transition" without destroying your software.

## 1. The Secret Backup (The Shadow Audit)
Do not tell the underperforming agency that you are unhappy yet. 
If they know they are about to be fired, they will stop trying entirely, and the quality of the final code will plummet. 

Instead, you must perform a "Shadow Audit." You secretly hire an independent Technical Consultant or your new offshore agency. You grant this new entity "Read-Only" access to your GitHub repository and AWS servers. 

The new team's job is to quietly map out the entire architecture, identify exactly where the terrible code is hidden, and verify that the database backups are actually functioning. 

## 2. Securing the "Keys to the Kingdom"
The number one reason companies get trapped by bad agencies is because the agency legally or technically owns the infrastructure. 

Before you fire them, you must ensure you possess absolute "Master Admin" control over:
* The GitHub / Source Code Repository.
* The AWS / Google Cloud hosting accounts.
* The Domain Name Registrar (GoDaddy, Route53).
* The Apple App Store / Google Play Developer accounts.

If the offshore agency created the AWS account under *their* corporate email, you do not own the servers. You must quietly force them to transfer Master Admin rights to your corporate email address under the guise of an "internal security audit." 

## 3. The "Knowledge Transfer" Phase
Once you have secured the infrastructure, you initiate the breakup. 
However, you do not fire them instantly. You invoke the "Transition Clause" in your Master Services Agreement (MSA). 

You explicitly mandate a 2-to-4-week "Knowledge Transfer" (KT) period. During this period, the old agency stops writing new code. Their only job is to sit on recorded Zoom calls with your *new* development team and explain exactly how the complex, undocumented parts of the software work. You pay the old agency their full hourly rate for these meetings. It is the cheapest insurance policy you will ever buy.

## 4. The Final Cutover
On the final day of the contract, the cutover must be swift and absolute. 
At exactly 5:00 PM:
1. You revoke the old agency's access to GitHub.
2. You revoke their access to AWS and change all master database passwords.
3. You remove them from your corporate Slack channels. 

A failed software outsourcing relationship is painful, but it is rarely fatal if handled correctly. By treating the vendor transition as a highly orchestrated security operation, you can safely extract your Intellectual Property and hand it over to a premium, capable development center without missing a single day of business operations.

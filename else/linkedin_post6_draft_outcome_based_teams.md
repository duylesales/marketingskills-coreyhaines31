# LinkedIn Post #6: Stop Tracking Hours: The Outcome-Based Remote Team

**Publish Date:** Week 3 (November 17)
**Format:** Text post
**Goal:** Thought leadership, Engagement

---

⏱️ If you are managing an offshore development team by tracking their hours, you are doing it wrong.

In 2026, the best engineering teams operate on an "Outcome-Based" model. 

Here is what we've learned managing distributed teams between Europe and Vietnam:

❌ Time-tracking creates a culture of looking busy.
✅ Outcome-tracking creates a culture of shipping value.

How to make the shift:
1️⃣ Adopt the DRI Model (Directly Responsible Individual) for every feature.
2️⃣ Move to Async-First communication. Stop demanding immediate Slack replies; protect deep work focus time.
3️⃣ Measure PRs merged, sprint goals hit, and cycle time—not keyboard strokes.

When you treat offshore developers like code monkeys, you get code monkeys.
When you treat them like engineering partners, they build incredible products.

How do you measure the success of your remote teams?

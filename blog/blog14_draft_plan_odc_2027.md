## The December Planning Timeline: Path to Q1 Readiness

If your goal is to have an offshore team executing your roadmap in January, here is the exact schedule you must follow in December.

### Week 1 (Dec 1-7): Define the "Core vs. Context" Strategy
You cannot outsource technical leadership if you do not have a clear vision. 
* **Action:** Identify what stays in-house (The Core) and what moves offshore (The Context).
* **Best Practice:** Keep your Product Owners and Lead Architects in-house. Offshore the execution: Frontend/Backend Developers, QA Automation Engineers, and DevOps executors.
* **Deliverable:** A clear Job Description (JD) and technical requirements document for the initial 3-5 offshore roles you need to fill.

### Week 2 (Dec 8-14): Vendor Selection & Legal Framework
Do not attempt to set up a legal entity in a foreign country yourself (the BOT model) unless you plan to hire 50+ developers. For most mid-market companies, partnering with an established ODC vendor is vastly superior.
* **Action:** Interview ODC vendors. Focus on their recruitment speed, developer retention rates, and security certifications (ISO 27001).
* **The Legal Foundation:** Finalize the Master Services Agreement (MSA) and Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs). Ensure the contract explicitly states that you own 100% of the Intellectual Property (IP) and source code from day one.
* **Deliverable:** Signed MSA with your chosen ODC partner (like **Manifera**).

### Week 3 (Dec 15-21): Infrastructure & Security Setup
Before a single developer is hired, the digital workspace must be secure.
* **Action:** Set up the Zero-Trust architecture. Create Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) profiles in your AWS/Azure environments. Configure VPNs or Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI) if required by your compliance frameworks (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA).
* **Communication:** Set up dedicated Slack/Teams channels and grant access to Jira/Confluence.
* **Deliverable:** A secure, partitioned environment ready to welcome external developers without exposing your entire corporate network.

### Week 4 (Dec 22-31): Candidate Interviews & Final Selection
While you were handling legal and infrastructure in Weeks 2-3, your ODC partner was sourcing and pre-vetting candidates.
* **Action:** Conduct final technical and cultural interviews with the shortlisted candidates. 
* **The Golden Rule:** You must have the final say on who joins your dedicated team. Never accept a "black box" team assigned to you without your approval.
* **Deliverable:** Signed offers for the founding members of your offshore team.

## The Q1 Execution: The First 30 Days

By following the December timeline, your team is ready to start in January. The first 30 days are critical for setting the cultural tone and velocity of the ODC.

### 1. The Async-First Onboarding
Do not expect the offshore team to intuitively understand your monolithic, undocumented legacy codebase. 
* You must invest heavily in onboarding documentation. Record Loom videos explaining the architecture. 
* Assign a dedicated "Buddy" from your in-house team to answer questions during the overlap hours.

### 2. Define the "Definition of Done"
Misalignment on quality is the #1 killer of offshore teams.
* Explicitly define what constitutes a finished task. Does it require 80% unit test coverage? Must it pass a SonarQube security scan? Does it require documentation? Put this in writing.

### 3. Start Small to Build Trust
Do not assign the offshore team the most critical, high-risk feature on Day 1. 
* Start them on low-risk bugs, technical debt cleanup, or isolated microservices. This allows them to learn your CI/CD pipelines, code review standards, and communication style without jeopardizing the Q1 launch.

## 1. The "Named Resources" Clause

**What is the Bait and Switch Scam?**  
The Bait and Switch is a deceptive outsourcing practice where an agency uses elite, highly experienced engineers to win a client contract during the sales phase, but secretly replaces them with cheap, inexperienced junior developers the moment the contract is signed to maximize profit margins.

The greatest weapon an offshore agency has is ambiguity. If the contract says: *"The Agency will provide 1 Lead Engineer and 3 Developers,"* they are legally allowed to swap human beings at any time.

**The Elite Mandate: Named Resources**  
Elite procurement teams do not accept generic titles. They demand a **"Named Resources"** clause. The contract must explicitly state the legal names of the human beings assigned to the project. Furthermore, the contract must contain a **Key Personnel Lock-In**. The agency is legally forbidden from removing Named Resources from the project without written notice and the client's explicit approval.

## 2. The Technical Interrogation (Bypassing the Sales Shield)

**Why Conduct a Technical Interrogation?**  
A technical interrogation bypasses the agency's polished sales team and allows the client's internal CTO to directly interview the exact offshore developers proposed for the project, ensuring their actual coding abilities match the senior-level claims made on their inflated resumes.

You cannot trust a resume provided by an amateur software development firm. Agencies frequently falsify resumes to bill junior developers at "Senior" rates. 

**The Elite Audit:**  
Demand a 60-minute technical interview with the exact developers who will write the code. Do not let the agency's Sales Director talk during this interview. 

Ask the proposed Senior Developer a terrifying, highly specific architectural question. If the developer panics or gives a generic textbook answer, they are not a Senior Developer. Reject them immediately. 

---

## 3. The "Code Commit" Velocity Audit

**What is Git Forensics?**  
Git Forensics is the practice of auditing a software repository's commit history to mathematically verify which specific developers are physically writing the code, ensuring that the senior engineers billed to the project are actually contributing rather than acting as absent figureheads.

Assume you successfully locked in the Named Resources. You still must verify that the Senior Developer is actually doing the work.

**The Elite Audit: Git Forensics**  
You must demand ownership of the Git repository. Every week, your internal CTO must look at the physical commit history. If the Senior Developer's name only appears on 2% of the code commits, and 98% of the code is being committed by unknown junior developers, the agency is executing a "Shadow Bait and Switch."

## Critical Requirements for FinTech Outsourcing

If you are outsourcing a FinTech project, "good code" is not enough. Your offshore partner must treat security and compliance as foundational, not as an afterthought.

### 1. Security Certifications & Frameworks
Your outsourcing partner should ideally hold an **ISO 27001** certification, proving they have an established Information Security Management System (ISMS). Furthermore, the software they build for you must comply with **PCI DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) if it handles card data, and **SOC 2 Type II** for general cloud security and privacy.

### 2. Data Encryption & Privacy (GDPR/CCPA)
Developers must implement end-to-end encryption. Data must be encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3 and at rest using AES-256. For European clients, the offshore team must understand the principles of "Privacy by Design" mandated by the GDPR, ensuring PII (Personally Identifiable Information) is anonymized in non-production environments.

### 3. Audit Trail Capabilities
Every financial transaction, configuration change, and user login must be logged securely and immutably. The offshore team must build robust audit trails (often using append-only databases) that can satisfy external regulatory audits.

### 4. Secure Software Development Lifecycle (SSDLC)
Security cannot be a phase at the end of the project. It must be integrated into the CI/CD pipeline via automated Static Application Security Testing (SAST), Dynamic Application Security Testing (DAST), and regular Dependency Scanning to prevent supply chain attacks.

## How to Evaluate a FinTech Outsourcing Partner

When interviewing potential offshore partners for a financial application, use this 10-point checklist:

1. **Domain Expertise:** Have they built financial applications before, or are you paying them to learn FinTech on your dime?
2. **Zero-Trust Environments:** Do their developers work on secure, monitored networks with Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?
3. **Data Handling:** Can they prove that their developers never download production PII data to local machines?
4. **Code Ownership:** Does the contract explicitly state that you own 100% of the intellectual property and source code?
5. **Team Stability:** FinTech requires deep context. What is their developer retention rate? (High turnover is a massive security risk).
6. **Vulnerability Response:** What is their SLA for patching critical zero-day vulnerabilities (like Log4j) in your dependencies?
7. **Testing Culture:** Do they enforce strict Unit Test coverage thresholds (e.g., 80%+) for all financial logic?
8. **Compliance Knowledge:** Are their architects familiar with PSD2, GDPR, or specific financial regulations in your target market?
9. **Penetration Testing:** Do they facilitate third-party penetration testing before major releases?
10. **Cultural Alignment:** Will they speak up and warn you if a feature request introduces a security risk, or will they blindly build it?

## FAQ

**Q: Is it legally permissible to use offshore developers for European financial software?**  
A: Yes, provided the vendor complies with GDPR and specific financial regulations (like DORA in the EU). The key is ensuring that data residency remains in the EU and that the offshore team accesses the data via secure, encrypted channels without storing PII locally.

**Q: How do we ensure the offshore team doesn't steal our proprietary trading algorithms or financial logic?**  
A: Strong legal protections (NDAs, IP transfer clauses in the Master Services Agreement) combined with technical controls. At Manifera, we use Zero-Trust environments, restrict code repository access, and ensure developers only have access to the specific modules they are working on.

**Q: What is the best engagement model for a FinTech project?**

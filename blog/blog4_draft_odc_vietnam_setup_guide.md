### 3. Government "Make in Vietnam" Initiatives

## 3 ODC Models Available in Vietnam

Before hiring a single developer, you must decide how your ODC will operate. There are three primary models to choose from.

### Model 1: Dedicated Teams via a Partner (The Fastest Setup)
In this model, you partner with an established IT company in Vietnam (like Manifera). The partner recruits, houses, and administers a team of developers who work exclusively for you. 
* **Pros:** Setup takes weeks, not months. Zero legal entity registration required. Partner handles HR, payroll, and office space.
* **Cons:** You pay a management fee to the partner.

### Model 2: Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT)
A partner builds the team and operates it for a set period (usually 12-24 months). After the team is stable and processes are refined, the entire operation (including the developers and the legal entity) is "transferred" to your full ownership.
* **Pros:** Low initial risk with the ultimate reward of full ownership.
* **Cons:** Requires a long-term commitment and eventual legal setup.

### Model 3: Wholly Owned Subsidiary (Full Ownership)
You register a legal entity in Vietnam, rent an office, hire HR, and recruit developers entirely on your own.
* **Pros:** 100% control over culture and operations. No vendor margins.
* **Cons:** Setup can take 6-12 months. High initial CapEx. Requires deep knowledge of local labor laws.

**Comparison Table**

| Model | Timeline to Launch | Initial Cost (CapEx) | Control | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dedicated Team (Partner)** | 2-4 weeks | Low | High (over daily tasks) | Low |
| **Build-Operate-Transfer** | 4-8 weeks | Medium | High | Medium |

*For 80% of companies starting in Vietnam, the **Dedicated Team model via a partner** is the smartest choice for 2026, allowing for rapid scaling with minimal risk.*

## Step-by-Step Setup Process (Partner Model)

If you choose to set up an ODC via a dedicated team partner, the process is streamlined but requires careful planning.

### Step 1: Define Requirements & Team Structure
Don't just ask for "three senior developers." Define the exact roles (e.g., 1 Lead Architect, 2 Backend Node.js Devs, 1 Frontend React Dev, 1 QA Automation). Clarify the product roadmap for the next 12 months.

### Step 2: Choose Your ODC Partner
Look for partners with experience in your industry, strong retention rates, and transparent pricing. Crucially, look for a partner that bridges the cultural gap. Manifera, for example, uses Dutch management to ensure European business standards are seamlessly integrated with Vietnamese engineering talent.

### Step 3: Talent Acquisition & Interviewing
Your partner will source candidates, but you should conduct the final technical and cultural interviews. Treat these candidates exactly as you would local hires.

### Step 4: Infrastructure & Tools Setup
Provision secure laptops, set up VPNs, and grant access to your Jira, GitHub, and Slack workspaces. Implement Zero-Trust architecture from day one.

### Step 5: Process Integration (Agile/Scrum)
Align the new team with your Agile ceremonies. Decide on sprint lengths, daily standup times (accounting for timezone overlap), and code review protocols.

### Step 6: Go Live & The 90-Day Ramp-up
Expect a learning curve. The first 30 days should focus on architecture absorption. By day 60, velocity should increase. By day 90, the ODC should be operating with full autonomy.

## Legal & Compliance Considerations

Protecting your Intellectual Property and remaining compliant with international data laws is paramount.

* **IP Protection:** Ensure your vendor contract clearly states that all IP, source code, and assets developed by the ODC are 100% owned by your company. Vietnam is a signatory to major international IP treaties.
* **GDPR & Data Privacy:** If you are a European company, your ODC partner must enforce GDPR compliance. This includes secure data handling, non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) signed by all developers, and strict access controls.
* **Labor Laws:** Vietnamese labor law is highly protective of employees. If you use a partner like Manifera, we handle all compliance, contracts, health insurance, and the mandatory 13th-month bonus, shielding you from legal liabilities.

## Cultural Integration: Making It Work

An ODC fails when it is treated as a "code factory." It succeeds when it is treated as a true extension of your company.

* **Communication:** Vietnamese developers are technically brilliant but may sometimes hesitate to challenge a senior manager directly due to a culture of respect for hierarchy. Encourage open feedback and emphasize that asking questions is expected.
* **The Bridge:** This is where Manifera excels. With our Dutch-Vietnamese bridge approach, our European managers in Asia help translate not just the language, but the business context and cultural nuances between the local team and your headquarters.
* **Timezone Management:** Schedule your daily scrums during the overlapping hours (e.g., 9:00 AM CET is 3:00 PM in Vietnam). Use async communication tools effectively for the rest of the day.

## 7 Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Treating them as "The B-Team":** Give your ODC challenging architectural work, not just bug fixes.
2. **Skipping Onboarding:** Throwing them a Jira ticket on Day 1 without explaining the product vision will lead to misaligned code.
3. **Tracking Hours Instead of Outcomes:** Measure pull requests merged and features shipped, not hours logged.
4. **Poor Documentation:** If your architecture isn't documented, your offshore team will have to guess.
5. **Ignoring the Timezone:** Don't schedule mandatory meetings at 10:00 PM Vietnam time.
6. **Bargain Hunting:** Don't sacrifice quality for the absolute lowest rate. A cheap developer who writes tech debt is vastly more expensive than a premium developer who writes clean code.
7. **Neglecting Face-to-Face Time:** If possible, fly your lead offshore developers to your HQ once a year, or visit the ODC. It builds immense loyalty.

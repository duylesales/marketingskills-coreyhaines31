## What Is a Dedicated Team Model?

A dedicated team is a **full-time, exclusive group of developers, QA engineers, and project managers** who work solely on your project. They operate as a natural extension of your in-house team, sharing your tools, processes, and product vision.

Unlike freelancers or short-term contractors, a dedicated team integrates deeply into your organization:

- They attend your daily standups and sprint planning sessions
- They learn your domain, business logic, and codebase over months and years
- They are managed through a clear structure — often with a Technical Lead and an Account Manager on the vendor side

**At Manifera**, our dedicated teams follow a Dutch-managed, Vietnamese-engineered model: European-standard project management combined with Vietnam's deep pool of skilled engineers. This hybrid approach delivers the communication quality of a local partner with the cost efficiency of offshore development.

**Best for:** Long-term product development, complex systems, R&D initiatives, and companies building proprietary technology.

## What Is Staff Augmentation?

Staff augmentation is a model where you **hire individual specialists on a temporary basis** to fill specific gaps in your existing team. These professionals work under your direct management, following your processes and using your tools.

Think of it as "renting" expertise:

- Need a React Native specialist for 3 months? Staff augmentation.
- Your senior backend engineer is on parental leave? Staff augmentation.
- Tight deadline requires 2 extra QA engineers? Staff augmentation.

The key difference: **you manage these individuals directly.** They plug into your team structure rather than operating as an independent unit.

**Best for:** Short-term skill gaps, peak workload periods, specific expertise needs, and companies with strong in-house technical leadership.

## When to Choose a Dedicated Team: 5 Clear Signals

If you recognize three or more of these signals, a dedicated team is likely your best option:

**1. You are building a product, not running a project.**
Products evolve continuously. They need a team that understands the full context — the architecture decisions made 6 months ago, the technical debt that was intentionally deferred, the user feedback that shaped the last release.

**2. You need deep domain expertise.**
FinTech, HealthTech, logistics — these industries require developers who understand the regulatory landscape and can make informed technical decisions. A rotating cast of contractors will never develop this depth.

**3. You want to scale capacity without scaling management overhead.**
With a dedicated team from a partner like **Manifera**, you get a self-organizing unit with its own Technical Lead. You set the direction; the team handles execution.

**4. Developer retention matters to your velocity.**
Every time a contractor leaves, you lose 2-3 months of institutional knowledge. Dedicated teams solve this: retention rates at established offshore partners like **Manifera** exceed 90% annually.

**5. You need seamless Agile integration.**
Dedicated teams participate in every ceremony — standups, retrospectives, sprint reviews. They become indistinguishable from your in-house team over time.

## When to Choose Staff Augmentation: 5 Clear Signals

Staff augmentation is the right choice when:

**1. You have a specific, time-boxed skill gap.**
Your team needs a machine learning engineer for 4 months to build a recommendation engine. Once it ships, the need disappears.

**2. Your in-house team is strong but stretched.**
You have capable technical leadership and management capacity — you just need more hands temporarily.

**3. The project has a clear end date.**
A fixed-scope project with a 3-month deadline is ideal for augmented staff.

**4. Budget is limited and commitment needs to be flexible.**
You can scale individual contractors up or down week by week, without the commitment of a full team retainer.

**5. You do not need long-term knowledge transfer.**
The work is self-contained and does not require deep understanding of your broader system architecture.

## The Hidden Costs of Choosing Wrong

The most expensive decision is not choosing the cheapest option — it is choosing the wrong *model.*

### Scenario A: Using Staff Augmentation for a Long-Term Product

**What happens:**
- Contractor A builds Feature X, then leaves after 3 months
- Contractor B arrives, spends 6 weeks understanding Contractor A's code
- Contractor B refactors 40% of Feature X before building Feature Y
- Contractor B's contract ends. Contractor C arrives...

**The result:** You pay 3 different people to understand the same codebase. Each transition costs you 6-8 weeks of lost productivity. After 12 months, you have spent **2x the budget** of a dedicated team and delivered **half the features.**

### Scenario B: Using a Dedicated Team for a 6-Week Sprint

**What happens:**
- You commit to a monthly retainer for a 5-person team
- The project completes in 6 weeks
- You now have a team sitting idle while you figure out the next project
- You pay 2 months of retainer for 1.5 months of productive work

**The result:** You overspend on capacity you do not need. Staff augmentation with 2-3 specialists would have cost 40% less.

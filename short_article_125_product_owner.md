# The Role of a Product Owner in Agile Outsourcing

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** software product owner, agile outsourcing, IT project management

When an enterprise company decides to outsource a major software project to an offshore development center, the CEO usually breathes a sigh of relief. *"Great, we hired the agency. Now they will build the software, and we can just wait for them to deliver it."*

This is the exact mindset that causes 50% of outsourcing projects to fail. 

You can outsource the coding. You can outsource the QA testing. You can outsource the server architecture. But you can **never** completely outsource the business vision. 

To bridge the massive gap between the company's business goals and the offshore developers writing the code, there is one non-negotiable role that must be filled: **The Product Owner (PO).**

## What is a Product Owner?
In the Agile Scrum methodology, the Product Owner is the single point of truth for what the software is supposed to do. 
The developers do not decide what features to build; they just execute the code. The Scrum Master does not decide what features to build; they just keep the team organized. 

The Product Owner decides the **"What"** and the **"Why."** 

The PO holds the vision. They prioritize the "Product Backlog" (the master list of all features). If the company only has the budget to build either a "Live Chat" feature or a "PDF Export" feature this month, the developers cannot make that choice. The Product Owner makes that choice based on customer feedback and business value.

## The Danger of the "Missing" Product Owner
If a company hires an offshore agency in Vietnam but fails to assign a dedicated, empowered Product Owner internally, the project descends into chaos. 

When offshore developers run into a roadblock, they need an answer immediately. 
*Developer: "If a user forgets their password, should we send them a text message or an email?"*

If there is no PO, the developer has to email the CEO. The CEO is busy running the company and doesn't reply for four days. The developer is blocked. The project stalls. Or worse, the developer just guesses, builds the wrong thing, and wastes thousands of dollars. 

## Internal vs. Proxy Product Owners
So, who should the Product Owner be?

**Option A: The Internal PO (The Gold Standard)**
Ideally, the Product Owner is someone inside your company. It should be a senior manager who deeply understands your customers, has the authority to make immediate financial/feature decisions, and has 10–15 hours a week dedicated entirely to meeting with the offshore development team. 

**Option B: The Proxy PO (The Agency Solution)**
If your company simply does not have a tech-savvy executive with 15 hours of free time, you must hire a **Proxy Product Owner** from the offshore agency. 
This is a highly skilled Business Analyst provided by the agency. They embed themselves into your company, interview your stakeholders, and act as the "translator." They absorb your high-level business goals and translate them into hyper-detailed technical Jira tickets for the developers. 

Even with a Proxy PO, your company must still provide an internal stakeholder to give final approvals. 

A premium offshore development center can write brilliant code, but code without a captain is just expensive typing. The Product Owner is the captain steering the ship, ensuring that every dollar spent offshore is directly generating business value.

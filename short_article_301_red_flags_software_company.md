# 5 Red Flags During the First Sales Call With a Software Development Company

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** software development company, B2B software agency, hire custom development company

You are the CEO of a rapidly growing logistics firm. You need a custom software platform to manage your global shipping fleet. You schedule a discovery call with a potential **software development company**. 

The agency has a beautiful website, glowing testimonials, and a charismatic sales director. 

During the one-hour Zoom call, everything sounds perfect. They promise to build exactly what you want, faster than you expected, and under budget. You are thrilled. 

But if you are a veteran of custom software development, you know that a "perfect" sales call is often a terrifying illusion. Elite offshore development agencies do not just say "yes" to everything. They interrogate you. 

Before you sign a $150,000 contract, watch out for these 5 catastrophic red flags during your initial sales call with a software development company. 

## Red Flag #1: They Don't Ask "Why?"
A standard agency is an order-taker. If you say, *"I want a mobile app with a live GPS tracking map,"* they will immediately say, *"Great! That will cost $40,000."*

An elite software development company is a strategic partner. If you ask for a live GPS map, the Lead Architect will stop you and ask: *"Why do you need a mobile app? Are your truck drivers actually going to download an app from the App Store, or would a mobile-friendly Web App be faster and cheaper? Why does the GPS need to be 'live' every second instead of updating every 5 minutes to save battery life?"*

If the agency does not aggressively push back on your assumptions and ask about your underlying business goals, they are just trying to sell you code, not a solution. 

## Red Flag #2: The "Yes to Everything" Trap
No software development company is an expert in everything. 

If you ask the agency, *"Do you do React? Do you do Python? Do you do Blockchain? Do you do AI? Do you do hardware integration?"* and the sales director confidently answers **"Yes"** to all of them, run away. 

Premium agencies have strict mathematical specialties. An elite agency will proudly admit: *"We are world-class at Node.js and React web platforms. We do not do blockchain. We do not do native iOS apps. If you need blockchain, you should hire someone else."* Honesty about limitations is the ultimate sign of technical competence. 

## Red Flag #3: Instant Price Quotes
Custom software is an infinitely complex mathematical puzzle. 

If a software development company gives you a precise financial quote (e.g., *"This CRM will cost exactly $65,500"*) after a 30-minute conversation, they are lying to you. 

They are quoting a low number to win the contract, knowing they will trap you with "change orders" and hidden fees later. An honest agency will only give a rough, massive range (e.g., *"Between $80k and $120k"*) until they spend two weeks conducting a formal, paid Discovery and Architecture Phase. 

## Red Flag #4: No Talk of Maintenance
Amateurs talk about the launch date. Professionals talk about what happens three years after the launch date. 

If the software development company does not explicitly bring up long-term maintenance, server hosting costs, and security patching during the first call, they do not understand the lifecycle of enterprise software. 

## Red Flag #5: The Sales Rep Has No Technical Backup
If you ask a deep architectural question (e.g., *"How do you handle database tenant isolation?"*) and the sales director says, *"I'll have to ask my tech team,"* that is a bad sign. 

Elite offshore agencies bring a Senior Architect or Lead Engineer to the very first sales call. They want to ensure the technical foundation is possible before they even discuss pricing. 

When interviewing a software development company, you are not buying a product. You are getting married to an engineering team. Look for a partner who challenges you, admits their flaws, and obsesses over the long-term reality of your business.

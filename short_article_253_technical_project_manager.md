# Why You Need a Technical Project Manager (TPM) for Offshore Teams

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** offshore technical project manager, custom software project management, software development communication

A non-technical founder decides to build a B2B CRM. They hire a brilliant team of offshore developers in Vietnam. 

Because the founder wants to save money, they decide to manage the offshore team directly. 

On Monday, the founder sends an email to the Lead Developer: *"I need the CRM dashboard to show a massive graph of our yearly sales revenue. I need it by Friday."*

The offshore developer receives the email. They are confused. *Does the founder want the graph to include taxes? Does the graph need to update in real-time, or just once a day? Should it include canceled orders?* 

Because the developer is too polite (or too scared) to bother the CEO with twenty tiny mathematical questions, they make a guess. They spend five days building a massively complex, real-time graph that includes canceled orders but ignores taxes. 

On Friday, the CEO looks at the graph. It is completely wrong. The CEO is furious that the developers wasted a week. The developers are furious that the CEO's instructions were vague. 

This is the most common reason offshore software projects fail. The problem isn't bad code; it is a **Communication Chasm**. To bridge this chasm, elite offshore agencies mandate the inclusion of a **Technical Project Manager (TPM)**. 

## The Role of the TPM (The Translator)
A Technical Project Manager is the ultimate bilingual translator. They speak the chaotic, emotional language of "Business," and they speak the rigid, mathematical language of "Code." 

When the CEO says: *"I want a massive graph of yearly revenue,"* the TPM does not hand that instruction to the developers. 

The TPM intercepts the request and instantly translates it into a rigid mathematical blueprint called a **User Story**. The TPM writes a highly detailed ticket in Jira:

**User Story: Revenue Graph**
* **Data Source:** Pull only from the `Completed_Orders` table. Ignore `Canceled_Orders`. 
* **Calculation:** Revenue must exclude Sales Tax. 
* **Performance:** The graph does not need to be real-time. Calculate the math once every 24 hours at midnight to save server costs. 
* **Acceptance Criteria:** The graph must load in under 2 seconds. 

The TPM hands this flawless, mathematically precise ticket to the offshore developer. The developer doesn't have to guess. They just write the code exactly as instructed. 

## The Ultimate Shield Against "Scope Creep"
Non-technical founders are notorious for "Scope Creep." 
On Wednesday, the CEO randomly emails the developers: *"Hey, can we also make the graph animated, and maybe add a feature to export it as a PDF?"*

If developers are unmanaged, they will try to build the PDF feature, get distracted, and miss the Friday deadline for the main graph. 

The TPM acts as an impenetrable shield protecting the developers' focus. 
The TPM intercepts the CEO's email and says: *"That is a great idea. However, the developers are currently in a highly focused Agile Sprint to finish the graph by Friday. I will add the 'PDF Export' feature to our Backlog, and we will build it next week."*

The TPM protects the developers from the chaotic whims of the CEO, and protects the CEO from the slow, agonizing delays caused by developer confusion. 

If you are outsourcing a custom software project and the agency sends you a proposal that does not include a dedicated Technical Project Manager, reject the proposal. You are not buying code; you are buying a managed execution process. Without a TPM, you are buying chaos.

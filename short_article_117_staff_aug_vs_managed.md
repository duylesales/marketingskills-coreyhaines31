# The Difference Between IT Staff Augmentation and Managed Services

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** IT staff augmentation, managed IT services, offshore dedicated team

When a company realizes they do not have the internal engineering capacity to hit their software goals, they turn to outsourcing. 

However, "outsourcing" is a massive umbrella term. If you engage an offshore development agency, they will usually ask you: *"Do you want a Staff Augmentation model, or a Managed Services model?"*

Choosing the wrong model will completely derail your project. It dictates who manages the developers, who takes the risk, and how the contract is priced. Here is the difference, and how to choose the right model for your business.

## What is IT Staff Augmentation?
Staff Augmentation is the ultimate form of flexibility. In this model, you are essentially "renting" elite offshore developers to temporarily join your existing internal team.

**How it Works:** 
You need two Senior React Developers. The offshore agency provides two highly vetted developers from their talent pool. Those developers instantly integrate into *your* team. They report to *your* CTO, they attend *your* Daily Standups, and they code under *your* direction. 

**The Pros:**
* **Total Control:** You retain 100% control over the project direction and the daily tasks.
* **Instant Scale:** If you need a DevOps engineer for just three months to configure an AWS server, you augment your team, use the engineer, and then release them. No HR recruiting nightmares.
* **Cultural Integration:** The offshore developers act as a direct extension of your local company culture.

**The Cons:**
* **You Bear the Risk:** Because *you* are managing the project, if the software is delayed because your local Product Manager wrote terrible requirements, the failure is entirely your fault. 

## What is Managed Services?
Managed Services (often called "Project Outsourcing") is the exact opposite. In this model, you are completely hands-off. 

**How it Works:**
You do not want to manage developers. You just want the finished software. You hand the offshore agency a comprehensive blueprint of your desired application. The agency provides the entire "pod"—the Product Manager, the QA testers, the Tech Lead, and the Developers. 

The agency's Product Manager runs the project. You only check in for weekly or bi-weekly milestone reviews to see the progress. 

**The Pros:**
* **Zero Management Overhead:** Your internal CTO and managers are completely free to focus on other business initiatives. The agency handles the daily chaos of software development.
* **Risk Transfer:** The agency usually guarantees delivery based on specific SLAs (Service Level Agreements). If the QA testing fails, it is the agency's responsibility to fix it.

**The Cons:**
* **Less Flexibility:** Because the agency has committed to a specific delivery scope, you cannot suddenly change your mind halfway through the project and ask the developers to build a completely different feature without triggering a massive contract renegotiation.

## Which Should You Choose?
The decision comes down to your internal technical leadership. 

If you have a strong internal CTO, great Product Managers, and a mature Agile process, **Staff Augmentation** is the superior choice. It allows you to rapidly inject elite offshore talent into your robust system.

If your company has zero internal tech leadership—perhaps you are a healthcare company with no in-house software expertise—you must use **Managed Services**. You need the offshore agency to provide the architectural leadership that your company lacks.

# How to Outsource Custom CRM Development vs Customizing Salesforce

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** outsource custom CRM development, offshore Salesforce customization, build vs buy CRM B2B

A mid-sized manufacturing company realizes their sales team is using Excel spreadsheets to track $50 million in annual B2B deals. It is total chaos. 

The CEO decides they need a CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system. They face the ultimate "Build vs Buy" dilemma:
1. **Option A:** Pay Salesforce $100,000 a year in software licenses, and pay an offshore agency to customize Salesforce. 
2. **Option B:** Refuse to pay Salesforce a dime, and pay an offshore development center $150,000 to build a completely custom, proprietary CRM from scratch. 

Many CEOs choose Option B because they hate the idea of paying a massive $100,000 recurring licensing fee to Salesforce forever. They believe building their own CRM will be "cheaper in the long run." 

This is almost always a catastrophic, million-dollar mistake. 

## The Illusion of the "Simple" CRM
Building a basic CRM is incredibly easy. An offshore junior developer can build a database that holds a customer's name, email, and phone number in two days. 

But a modern enterprise CRM is not a Rolodex. 
Within six months, the manufacturing sales team will demand:
* *"We need the CRM to automatically sync with our Microsoft Outlook emails."*
* *"We need the CRM to track when a customer opens a PDF proposal."*
* *"We need an iOS app so we can scan business cards at a trade show."*
* *"We need complex Role-Based Access Control so the Junior Sales Rep can't see the CEO's deals."*

If you are paying an offshore agency to build all of those massive, interconnected global features from absolute scratch, your $150,000 budget will instantly explode to $1.5 million. You are forcing an offshore team to try and invent a product that took Salesforce twenty years and a billion dollars to build. 

## When to Customize Salesforce (The "Buy" Strategy)
For 95% of businesses, the correct strategy is to buy a massive platform like Salesforce or HubSpot, and use an offshore agency purely for **Customization and Integration**. 

Salesforce is essentially a massive box of incredibly powerful Lego blocks. You do not want your offshore agency to invent new plastic; you just want them to snap the blocks together to fit your specific manufacturing business model. 

You hire an **Offshore Salesforce Apex Developer**. 
Instead of spending 6 months building a login system and an email syncing engine from scratch, the offshore Salesforce developer spends 3 weeks writing custom "Apex Code" that violently bends the massive Salesforce architecture to your exact will. 

You get the power of a billion-dollar platform, heavily customized for your niche, at a fraction of the custom development cost. 

## The 5% Exception: When to Build Custom
When is it actually a good idea to build a 100% custom CRM from scratch?

**1. You are the Product:** If your core business model is *selling* CRM software to other people (like you are building the next Mailchimp or Pipedrive), you obviously must own the IP and build it from scratch. 
**2. Bizarre Industry Compliance:** If you are operating in a hyper-niche industry (like military contractor logistics or extreme offshore oil drilling) where standard cloud software violates federal security regulations, you may be legally forced to build a proprietary CRM that you can host on private, underground servers. 
**3. The Massive Scale Penalty:** If you have 10,000 sales reps, paying Salesforce $150 per user, per month is $1.5 million a month. At that astronomical scale, paying an offshore agency $2 million to build a proprietary system becomes mathematically cheaper. 

If you are a standard B2B company trying to organize your sales team, do not reinvent the wheel. Pay the Salesforce tax, and hire an elite offshore agency to customize it.

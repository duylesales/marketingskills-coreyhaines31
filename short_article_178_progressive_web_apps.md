# The Business Case for Progressive Web Apps (PWAs)

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** progressive web apps, PWA development outsourcing, custom mobile app alternatives

A mid-market retail company decides they need a mobile app to increase customer loyalty. 

They hire an offshore development center to build a "Native" iOS app for Apple and a "Native" Android app for Google. They spend $150,000 and wait six months. 

When the apps launch, the company discovers a horrific reality: Customers refuse to download it. 
To get the app, a customer must go to the App Store, search for the brand, type in their Apple ID password, wait for a 50MB download, and open the app. That is a massive wall of friction. Most users simply abandon the process and go to a competitor's mobile website instead.

If your business does not actually require a native mobile app (like a complex 3D video game), spending $150,000 on App Store development is a massive mistake. The modern, highly lucrative alternative is outsourcing the development of a **Progressive Web App (PWA)**. 

## What is a Progressive Web App (PWA)?
A PWA is a hybrid. It is built using standard web technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React), but it looks, feels, and acts exactly like a native iPhone app. 

When a user visits your PWA website on their mobile browser, they don't have to go to the App Store. A small prompt appears: *"Add to Home Screen."*
With one tap, the website instantly installs itself onto the user's phone as an app icon. No App Store, no passwords, no massive download.

## 1. Bypassing the 30% App Store Tax
If you sell digital goods or subscriptions through a native iOS app, Apple takes a mandatory 30% cut of your gross revenue. This "App Store Tax" destroys profit margins. 

Because a PWA is technically a website, it completely bypasses the App Store. Apple cannot tax transactions that happen on the web. You keep 100% of your revenue. 

## 2. Slashing Development Costs in Half
If you build Native apps, you have to hire two separate teams: A Swift team for Apple, and a Kotlin team for Android. You are paying for two separate codebases. When you want to add a new feature, you have to pay to develop it twice. 

With a PWA, your offshore agency builds **one single codebase**. That one codebase works flawlessly on Chrome, Safari, iOS, Android, and Desktop. You have instantly cut your development and maintenance costs by 50%. 

## 3. Offline Capabilities and Push Notifications
Historically, the argument against web apps was that they required an internet connection. 

PWAs fix this using a technology called "Service Workers." A Service Worker silently caches the data on the user's phone. If your customer loses cell service while riding the subway, your PWA still opens instantly, and they can still browse the cached product catalog perfectly. 

Furthermore, PWAs support Push Notifications on Android (and increasingly on iOS). You can send an alert directly to the user's lock screen, driving massive re-engagement, just like a native app. 

## When You Still Need a Native App
PWAs are not perfect for everything. If your software requires deep, aggressive access to the phone's core hardware (like the Bluetooth chip, complex gyroscope data, or heavy graphical processing for gaming), you must build a Native app. 

But for e-commerce stores, B2B SaaS dashboards, news publications, and internal corporate tools, a Native app is total overkill. By instructing your offshore development team to build a PWA, you bypass the App Store friction, slash your development costs, and deliver a blazingly fast mobile experience to your users.

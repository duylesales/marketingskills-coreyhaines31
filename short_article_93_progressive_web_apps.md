# What is a Progressive Web App (PWA)?

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** progressive web app, PWA development, mobile web vs native app

For the last decade, companies faced a difficult binary choice when building a digital product. Do we build a Website (cheap, accessible everywhere, but clunky on a phone) or do we build a Native Mobile App (expensive, has to be downloaded from the App Store, but provides a beautiful mobile experience)?

Today, there is a third option that is rapidly becoming the industry standard for enterprise businesses: the **Progressive Web App (PWA)**. 

A PWA gives you the best of both worlds. Here is how it works and why you should consider building one.

## What is a PWA?
A Progressive Web App is simply a website that has been engineered to look, feel, and act exactly like a native mobile app. 

When a user visits a PWA in their mobile browser (like Safari or Chrome), they do not have to go to the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. Instead, their browser simply asks, "Add to Home Screen?" 

If they click yes, an icon appears on their phone's home screen. When they click that icon, the application opens in full screen (no URL bar, no browser navigation buttons). It feels exactly like a downloaded app.

## The Massive Advantages of a PWA

### 1. Bypassing the App Store Tax
If you build a Native iOS App, you must submit it to Apple for approval. Apple can reject your app for arbitrary reasons, delaying your launch by weeks. Furthermore, if you sell digital goods, Apple takes a massive 30% cut of your revenue. 
Because a PWA is technically just a website, you completely bypass the App Store. You launch the update whenever you want, and you keep 100% of your revenue.

### 2. Offline Functionality
Standard websites break immediately if you lose cell service. PWAs use a technology called "Service Workers." These are tiny scripts that run in the background and cache (save) data on the user's phone. 
If a user is on an airplane with no Wi-Fi, they can still open your PWA, read content, and interact with the app. When they land and get a signal, the PWA automatically syncs the offline data back to your servers. 

### 3. One Codebase to Rule Them All
If you build Native apps, you have to hire a Swift developer for the iPhone app, a Kotlin developer for the Android app, and a React developer for the desktop website. You are paying for three different teams. 
With a PWA, you pay one team of web developers to write one single codebase. That exact same code works perfectly on Desktop, iPhone, and Android, slashing your development costs by 60%.

## When NOT to use a PWA
While PWAs are incredible for B2B dashboards, e-commerce stores, and media sites, they have limits. Apple heavily restricts what PWAs can do on iPhones. For example, a PWA cannot easily access the phone's Bluetooth or advanced camera features. 

If you are building a complex 3D video game or an app that heavily relies on Bluetooth (like connecting to a heart rate monitor), you must build a Native App. But for 90% of business software, a PWA is the smartest, most cost-effective architectural choice.

## Cost by Engagement Model

The hourly rate is only half the equation. *How* you engage the outsourcing partner dramatically affects your total cost and risk profile.

### 1. Dedicated Team (Full-Time Equivalent / FTE)
You pay a fixed monthly retainer for a developer (or a team) to work 160 hours a month exclusively on your projects.
* **Pricing Structure:** Monthly Retainer (Developer Salary + Vendor Management Fee).
* **When it works:** For long-term projects, continuous product development, and scaling your in-house engineering capacity.
* **Cost Efficiency:** **Highest.** The hourly equivalent rate drops significantly because the vendor has guaranteed long-term revenue.

### 2. Time & Materials (T&M)
You pay strictly for the hours the developers spend working on your project, billed monthly.
* **Pricing Structure:** Hourly Rate × Hours Worked.
* **When it works:** For projects with undefined scope, ongoing maintenance, or highly agile environments where requirements change weekly.
* **Cost Efficiency:** **Medium.** You only pay for what you use, but the hourly rate is typically 15-25% higher than the FTE model.

### 3. Fixed Price
You agree on a fixed budget for a strictly defined scope of work before development begins.
* **Pricing Structure:** Lump sum paid in milestones (e.g., 30% upfront, 40% beta, 30% launch).
* **When it works:** For small, strictly defined MVPs or very specific module integrations.
* **Cost Efficiency:** **Lowest.** Vendors build a 20-30% "risk buffer" into the price to protect themselves against scope creep. If you change your mind mid-project, change requests will be expensive.

## Cost by Project Complexity

To give you a clearer picture of total budget requirements, here are estimated ranges for complete projects in 2026 (assuming a hybrid or offshore development team).

### Tier 1: Simple Applications ($20,000 - $80,000)
* **Examples:** Basic corporate websites, simple internal CRUD dashboards, Shopify storefront customizations, MVP mobile apps with no backend logic.

* **Timeline:** 3 to 6 months.

### Tier 3: Enterprise / Complex Systems ($250,000 - $1,000,000+)
* **Examples:** Large-scale cloud migrations, legacy system rebuilds, FinTech trading platforms, AI-integrated logistics engines.
* **Team Required:** 1 Architect, multiple Dedicated Scrum Teams (Frontend, Backend, DevOps, QA).
* **Timeline:** 6 to 18+ months.

## Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

When budgeting for outsourcing, the vendor's invoice is rarely the *total* cost. Beware of these hidden expenses:

1. **Communication Overhead:** If your internal team spends 10 hours a week clarifying requirements because the offshore team lacks English proficiency, that is a massive hidden cost.
2. **Quality Assurance & Rework:** A $25/hr developer who introduces bugs that take your in-house senior dev 5 hours to fix is more expensive than a $50/hr developer who writes clean code the first time.
3. **Ramp-up Time:** It takes time for an offshore team to learn your architecture. Expect velocity to be lower in the first 30-60 days.
4. **Infrastructure & Licensing:** Don't forget to budget for AWS/Azure staging environments, Jira seats, and GitHub Copilot licenses for the extended team.

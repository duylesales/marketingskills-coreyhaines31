🧱 The most expensive code your team writes today...
is the code they'll have to rewrite tomorrow.

We call it the "20% Rule" at Manifera:

Reserve 20% of every sprint for tech debt reduction.

Here's why it works:

→ Sprint 1-4: Feels "slow." You ship 80% of planned features.
→ Sprint 5-8: Tech debt decreases. Velocity catches up.  
→ Sprint 9-12: You're shipping FASTER than teams at 100% feature velocity.
→ Sprint 13+: Your codebase is cleaner. Onboarding takes days, not weeks.

The math is simple:
💰 Cost of the 20% rule: ~20% less features per sprint initially
💸 Cost of ignoring tech debt: 3-6 month rewrite in 18 months

We've seen this pattern in 160+ projects across 10 years.

The companies that invest in clean code today are the ones that scale 10x tomorrow.

What's your team's approach to managing tech debt?

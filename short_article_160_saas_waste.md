# Why You Should Stop Paying for Enterprise SaaS You Don't Use

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** enterprise SaaS waste, build vs buy software, custom internal tools

In the modern corporate world, buying Software as a Service (SaaS) is incredibly addictive. 

A department manager decides the team needs a better way to track marketing projects. Instead of asking the IT department, they simply take out a corporate credit card, sign up for Asana or Monday.com, and deploy it to their team of 20 people. 

Multiply this action across sales, HR, logistics, and finance, and you arrive at a horrifying statistic: The average mid-market enterprise company pays for over **130 different SaaS applications**, spending millions of dollars annually on subscription fees.

This phenomenon is known as "SaaS Sprawl," and it is quietly destroying corporate balance sheets. In 2026, smart CFOs are fighting back by auditing their software stacks and hiring offshore development centers to build **Custom Internal Tools**. Here is why renting software is often a massive financial mistake.

## 1. The "10% Utilization" Trap
When you sign a massive enterprise contract for Salesforce, HubSpot, or a massive ERP system, you are paying for the 100% full suite of features. 

The reality is that your employees probably only use 10% of those features. 
You are paying a premium price for complex AI forecasting, automated email cadences, and multi-currency billing engines—features your specific company will never turn on. You are essentially renting a 50-room mansion when your business only needs a 3-bedroom house. 

## 2. The Nightmare of Data Silos
When you have 130 different SaaS applications, your company data is shattered into 130 different pieces. 
* The Sales team’s data is trapped in Salesforce.
* The Support team’s data is trapped in Zendesk.
* The Marketing team’s data is trapped in Marketo.

When the CEO asks for a simple report showing how a marketing campaign impacted customer support wait times, it is impossible. The systems do not natively talk to each other. You have to hire expensive integration consultants just to force the leased software to communicate.

## 3. The Exponential "Per Seat" Tax
SaaS pricing models are designed to penalize growth. They almost always charge "Per Seat" (per user). 

If a piece of software costs $50 per user per month, and you have 100 employees, the cost is a manageable $60,000 a year.
If your company scales aggressively and you hire 1,000 employees, your bill instantly skyrockets to $600,000 a year for the exact same software. The SaaS vendor is taxing your company’s success without delivering any additional value. 

## The Custom Software Solution
The antidote to SaaS Sprawl is consolidating your core workflows into proprietary, custom-built internal tools. 

Imagine taking the $600,000 a year you waste on disjointed SaaS licenses, and handing $200,000 to a premium offshore development team in Vietnam.
* The offshore team builds a custom internal portal exactly tailored to your business. 
* It contains 100% of the features you actually need, and 0% of the bloated features you don't.
* The data sits in one unified, highly secure AWS database that your company owns. 
* Most importantly, you own the IP. You can add 5,000 new employees to the platform tomorrow, and your software cost increases by exactly zero dollars. 

You should absolutely "Buy" highly commoditized software (like Microsoft 365 for email or Slack for chat). But for the core operational workflows that define your competitive advantage, you must "Build." Escaping the SaaS licensing trap is the most reliable way to drastically improve your EBITDA margins.

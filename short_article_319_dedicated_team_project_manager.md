# How to Evaluate the Project Manager Leading Your Dedicated Development Team

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** dedicated development team, offshore project manager, offshore software scrum master

A manufacturing company hires a massive offshore agency to build a custom inventory management system. They sign a contract for a **Dedicated Development Team** consisting of 8 elite Software Engineers and 1 Project Manager (PM). 

The manufacturing CEO is thrilled with the resumes of the engineers. They all have Masters degrees in computer science and 10 years of Python experience. The CEO barely looks at the resume of the Project Manager. 

Three months into the project, the CEO realizes the software is completely off track. The engineers are building complex features that the factory floor doesn't actually need. The CEO sends furious emails to the offshore PM: *"Why are your engineers building the wrong thing?"*

The PM replies: *"I don't know, I just passed your PDF document to the engineers and told them to code it. I assumed you knew what you wanted."*

The CEO realizes they hired a highly paid secretary, not a Project Manager. 
In a Dedicated Development Team, the brilliant engineers are the engine. But the Project Manager is the steering wheel. If you have an 800-horsepower engine and a broken steering wheel, you will mathematically drive the project into a brick wall. 

When you interview an offshore agency, you must aggressively evaluate their Project Managers (or Scrum Masters) before you evaluate their code. 

## The "Ticket Passer" (The Secretary)
Amateur offshore agencies employ "Ticket Passers." 

A Ticket Passer is a glorified administrator. When you (the client) ask for a feature, the Ticket Passer literally copy-pastes your email into a Jira ticket and assigns it to a developer. They do not interrogate your request. They do not ask *why* you need the feature. They just pass the paper. 

If the developers are blocked by a technical issue, the Ticket Passer doesn't know how to help them, so they just update the spreadsheet to say the project is "Delayed." 

## The "Scrum Master / Product Owner" (The Translator)
Elite offshore development centers (like Manifera) do not employ Ticket Passers. They employ aggressive, highly technical translators. 

An elite offshore PM acts as an impenetrable shield between the chaotic business world and the hyper-logical engineering world. 

### 1. The Interrogation Phase
If you tell an elite PM, *"I want a button that exports the inventory to Excel,"* they do not just pass the ticket to the engineers. 

They interrogate you: *"Why Excel? Are your accountants manually manipulating the data? If they are, why don't we just build that mathematical manipulation directly into our dashboard so they don't have to use Excel at all?"* 

The PM protects you from your own bad ideas. 

### 2. The Un-Blocker
An elite PM does not just update spreadsheets. They actively hunt down bottlenecks. 
If an offshore engineer says, *"I can't build the login screen because the client hasn't given us the AWS database passwords,"* the elite PM does not just wait passively. They aggressively call the client, escalate the issue, and physically secure the passwords so the $100/hour engineer doesn't sit idle for two days. 

### 3. The Technical Translator
Engineers speak in mathematics (APIs, JSON, Latency). CEOs speak in business (ROI, Conversion Rates, Deadlines). 

The elite PM is perfectly fluent in both. They listen to the engineer explain a highly complex database index problem, and they translate it to the CEO: *"We need to delay the launch by 2 days, but if we do, the software will run 40% faster and save you $2,000 a month in server costs."*

When you hire a Dedicated Development Team, demand to interview the Project Manager first. Ask them: *"Tell me about a time you told a client their idea was terrible, and how you fixed it."* If they have never challenged a client, they are a secretary. Do not hire them.

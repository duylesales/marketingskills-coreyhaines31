# Fixed Price vs. Time & Materials: Software Pricing Models Explained

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** software pricing models, fixed price software, time and materials contract

When you hire an agency to build custom software, the first question you will ask is, "How much will this cost?" 

The agency will likely offer you two completely different pricing models: **Fixed Price** or **Time & Materials (T&M)**. Choosing the wrong model for your specific project can result in massive budget overruns, strained relationships, and a completely failed product launch.

Here is a guide to understanding software pricing models and how to choose the right one for your business.

## The Fixed Price Model
In a Fixed Price contract, the agency agrees to deliver a specific set of features for an exact, unchangeable dollar amount, by a specific deadline. 

### The Appeal:
To a CFO, this model sounds perfect. You know exactly what you are getting and exactly what it will cost. It minimizes financial risk.

### The Reality:
In software development, Fixed Price is often a trap. 
Software is inherently unpredictable. During development, you will inevitably look at a prototype and realize, "Actually, the users need this button to do something else."

In a Fixed Price contract, the agency has budgeted exactly $50,000 for the exact scope written in the contract. If you ask for a change, they will hit you with a massive "Change Request" fee. This creates an adversarial relationship where the agency is incentivized to cut corners to protect their profit margin, and you are penalized for trying to improve the product based on real-time feedback.

**Best For:** Very small, highly predictable projects (under $20,000) where the scope is 100% defined and guaranteed not to change.

## The Time & Materials (T&M) Model
In a Time & Materials contract, you pay the agency an hourly or monthly rate for the developers' time, regardless of how long the project takes. 

### The Appeal:
This model allows for true Agile development. Because you are paying for the team's time, you have total flexibility. If you realize halfway through the month that you want to scrap the original design and pivot the app to target a completely different market, you can just tell the team to pivot. There are no "Change Request" fees because you are directing the team's hours.

### The Reality:
To a CFO, this model feels risky. It feels like giving an agency a blank check. "What if they intentionally work slowly to bill more hours?"

This is why T&M contracts require extreme trust and transparency. You must demand access to the team's Jira boards and GitHub commits to verify that they are pushing code daily. 

**Best For:** Large, complex projects (startups, enterprise software) where the exact final product is impossible to predict on Day 1, and flexibility is more important than a rigid budget.

## The Modern Solution: The Dedicated Team Model
The most effective pricing model in 2026 is a variation of T&M: the **Dedicated Offshore Team**. 

Instead of paying varying hourly rates, you pay a flat, predictable monthly fee to "rent" a full-time, dedicated pod of offshore engineers (e.g., $15,000/month for a senior developer, a QA tester, and a PM). 
This gives the CFO a predictable monthly burn rate, while giving the Product Manager the infinite flexibility of a T&M model. The team acts as your own employees, fully aligned with your long-term success.

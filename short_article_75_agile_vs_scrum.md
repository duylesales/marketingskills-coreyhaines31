# Agile vs. Scrum: Understanding Software Methodologies

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** Agile vs Scrum, software development methodologies, agile team

If you spend five minutes talking to a software development agency, they will proudly tell you, "We use Agile and Scrum!" 

To someone outside the tech industry, these terms sound like meaningless corporate buzzwords used interchangeably. However, understanding the difference between Agile and Scrum is critical if you are going to successfully manage or hire a development team.

Here is the simple, non-jargon breakdown of Agile vs. Scrum.

## What is Agile? (The Philosophy)
Agile is not a specific set of rules. It is a philosophy or a mindset. 

Twenty years ago, software was built using the "Waterfall" method. You planned the entire application for six months, coded for a year, and then released it to the public. If the market hated the product, you had wasted 18 months and millions of dollars.

The Agile philosophy was invented to solve this. The core idea of Agile is: **Do not plan a year in advance. Build a tiny piece of the software quickly, show it to users, get their feedback, and adapt immediately.**

Agile values flexibility, human communication, and responding to change over following a rigid, unchangeable plan. 

## What is Scrum? (The Tool)
If Agile is the philosophy (like "eating healthy"), Scrum is the specific diet plan (like "Keto" or "Paleo") you use to achieve that philosophy. 

Scrum is a highly structured framework used to actually execute Agile principles on a daily basis. 

### The Rules of Scrum:
If a development team is using Scrum, they will follow these specific rules:

1. **The Sprint:** Work is not open-ended. It is broken into strict 2-week cycles called "Sprints." The team commits to a small amount of work, and they must finish it within those 14 days. 
2. **The Roles:** A Scrum team has strict roles. The **Product Owner** represents the client's business goals. The **Scrum Master** is essentially the referee who makes sure the team follows the rules and removes any roadblocks. The **Developers** do the actual work.
3. **The Daily Standup:** Every single morning, the team has a strict 15-minute meeting where everyone answers three questions: *What did I do yesterday? What am I doing today? Is anything blocking my progress?*
4. **The Sprint Review:** At the end of the 2-week Sprint, the team must demonstrate working, usable code to the stakeholders. 

## Why This Matters for Outsourcing
If you hire an offshore development team and they say they are "Agile," that is not enough. You must ask them *how* they implement Agile. 

If they do not use strict Scrum (or a similar framework like Kanban), "Agile" is just an excuse for them to be disorganized and miss deadlines. 

When partnering with an elite offshore development center, demand a team that operates with rigorous Scrum discipline. This ensures you have total transparency into their daily work, and you get to see tangible progress on your application every 14 days, rather than waiting months in the dark.

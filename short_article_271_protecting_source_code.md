# How to Protect Your Source Code When Changing Offshore Agencies

**Word Count:** ~600 words
**Target Keywords:** change offshore software agency, custom software source code transfer, offshore development vendor transition

A startup hires a cheap offshore development agency to build their Minimum Viable Product (MVP). The MVP is successful, and the startup raises $10 million in Series A funding. 

The startup’s new CTO decides the original offshore agency lacks the architectural expertise to scale the massive platform. The CTO fires the original agency and hires an elite, premium offshore development center in Vietnam. 

The original agency is furious. The day their contract is terminated, they maliciously change the master passwords to the Amazon AWS servers. They lock the startup out of their own database and refuse to surrender the source code until the startup pays a $50,000 "Transition Fee." 

The startup is legally extorted and forced to pay. 

When you outsource software development, you are surrendering massive operational control to a foreign entity. If the relationship sours, you must execute the "Vendor Transition" with military precision to protect your intellectual property. Here is how to fire an offshore agency securely. 

## 1. The "Pre-Firing" Security Audit (The Silent Lockout)
You never, ever tell an offshore agency they are being fired until you have mathematically severed their access to your critical infrastructure. 

Before you send the termination email, your internal CTO (or the new elite offshore agency you just hired) must execute a silent, coordinated **Access Revocation Protocol**. 

1. **Change the Root Passwords:** Log into Amazon AWS, Google Cloud, and your DNS provider. Immediately change the master "Root" passwords and enable Multi-Factor Authentication tied strictly to the CEO's cell phone. 
2. **Revoke GitHub Access:** Go to the Master Source Code Repository (GitHub or GitLab). Instantly downgrade every single developer from the original agency from "Admin" to "Read-Only," and then completely remove their access. 
3. **Seize the Domain Names:** Ensure the original agency does not control your GoDaddy or Namecheap accounts. If they control the web domain, they can legally shut down your live website in 3 seconds. 

Only after the digital locks have been changed do you send the termination email. 

## 2. The Comprehensive Knowledge Transfer (KT)
If you fire the original agency aggressively, they will not help you transition the knowledge. You will hand 1 million lines of complex code to your new elite agency, and the new agency will spend 3 months blindly guessing how the code works. 

If possible, you must negotiate a peaceful 30-day **Knowledge Transfer (KT) Period**. 

You pay the original agency for one final month. During this month, they write no new code. Their only job is to sit on Zoom calls with the new elite agency and mathematically explain the architecture. 

The new agency will aggressively interrogate the old agency:
* *"Where are the secret API keys stored?"*
* *"Explain the exact deployment script you use to launch the code to production."*
* *"Why did you use this weird database structure on line 400?"*

## 3. The Codebase Sterilization
Once the new elite offshore agency takes control, the first thing they must do is a **Codebase Sterilization Audit**. 

They scan the massive codebase looking for "Backdoors." 
A malicious (or just sloppy) fired developer might have left a secret, hardcoded admin password hidden deep in the source code (e.g., `if user == 'developer1', grant master access`). 

The new elite agency mathematically scrubs the entire database, invalidates every single active session, and deletes every old developer account from the system. 

Changing offshore agencies is open-heart surgery on your business. You must plan the transition secretly, secure the digital infrastructure aggressively, and hire an elite replacement agency that knows how to sterilize and inherit massive legacy systems.
